Our Story

Making Tennis Accessible to Every Kid

The Match Point Project is a student-led initiative dedicated to making tennis accessible to every kid. Our mission is to give back to our community by collecting spare tennis gear and money.

As passionate tennis players ourselves, we know firsthand the joy this sport brings — and we believe every kid deserves that same chance to play.

We strive to ensure the next generation will continue to be able to participate in tennis at the highest level. We aim to ensure no child is left on the sidelines due to the cost of equipment.

We're working with local Boys & Girls Club sites to get donated racquets directly into the hands of kids who can use them.

Frequently Asked Questions

Contact The Match Point Project at thematchpointproject.contact@gmail.com. We accept used racquets, tennis balls, and other gear in any condition. We clean and redistribute everything directly to young players who can't afford equipment, including through local Boys & Girls Club sites.

The Match Point Project is a student-led community initiative started by students at Leigh High School. We collect donated tennis gear and funds to make the sport accessible to every child, regardless of their financial situation.

A beginner tennis racquet costs $30-$100, and court fees, lessons, and balls add up quickly. For many families, this cost puts tennis out of reach — which is exactly why The Match Point Project collects and donates gear for free.

We collect used racquets, balls, and tennis equipment, clean them up, and give them directly to kids who need them, including by working with local Boys & Girls Club sites. No donation is too small — every piece of gear keeps a child on the court.

Students can support The Match Point Project by donating spare gear, spreading the word at their school or tennis club, or reaching out directly at thematchpointproject.contact@gmail.com to get involved.

Our Mission Statement

The Match Point Project is a student-led initiative from Leigh High School dedicated to making tennis accessible to every kid. Our mission is to give back to our community by collecting spare tennis gear and money.

As passionate tennis players ourselves, we know firsthand the joy this sport brings — and we believe every kid deserves that same chance to play.

We strive to ensure the next generation will continue to be able to participate in tennis at the highest level. We aim to ensure no child is left on the sidelines due to the cost of equipment.

By the end of the summer, we are planning to distribute these donations to organizations such as the Boys & Girls Club to help support youth tennis programs and get more people involved in the sport.
