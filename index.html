Frequently Asked Questions

Contact The Match Point Project at thematchpointproject.contact@gmail.com. We accept used racquets, tennis balls, and other gear in any condition. We clean and redistribute everything directly to young players in our community who can't afford equipment.

The Match Point Project is a student-led community initiative started by students at Leigh High School. We collect donated tennis gear and funds to make the sport accessible to every child, regardless of their financial situation.

A beginner tennis racquet costs $30–$100, and court fees, lessons, and balls add up quickly. For many families, this cost puts tennis out of reach — which is exactly why The Match Point Project collects and donates gear for free.

We collect used racquets, balls, and tennis equipment, clean them up, and give them directly to kids in our community who need them. No donation is too small — every piece of gear keeps a child on the court.

Students can support The Match Point Project by donating spare gear, spreading the word at their school or tennis club, or reaching out directly at thematchpointproject.contact@gmail.com to get involved with our initiative.
